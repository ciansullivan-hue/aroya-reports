WP4 → WP5 OTAP — what happens at the scheduled push

  • AROYA pushes WP5 over the air from the gateway. No site visit is required by default; the gateway flashes itself and propagates to every device online at the scheduled minute.
  • Go / no-go per device: online at push time, battery ≥ 30 %, signal (RSSI) ≥ 50 %.
  • Offline devices stay on WP4 — they are not bricked. They keep running on WP4 firmware. To bring them onto WP5 later, either (a) bring the device online before the next scheduled push, or (b) revert the gateway to WP4 temporarily and re-run the push.
  • Recovery is reversible: a gateway can be reverted to WP4 with no time limit, so stragglers can always be picked up later.
  • Battery-class devices (substrate nodes + TEROS-12 probes) are the only ones on battery; everything else (gateway, sink, repeaters, atm14 climate stations) is PoE. PoE devices generally come back online automatically with cable / power restored.
