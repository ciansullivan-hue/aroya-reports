2 · Measured field capacity — the anchor

The new methodology lives or dies on this number: what VWC does the substrate actually reach at peak each day? That's your real field capacity. Textbook ranges (rockwool 60-85%, coco 45-65%) only apply if you're hitting them. This room isn't.

Why this matters. If textbook says "target 60% proportional dryback" and your FC is 65%, you're dropping to 26%. If your FC is only 44%, that same 60% means dropping to 17.6% — which crashes through your 25% panic floor and risks coco's permanent-wilt zone. The same dryback target implies a different VWC floor for every room.

6 · CCMS notes

The steering-service/ repo (Jason Van Leuven's CCMS work) is wired to Donner Party 3217 / Long Skinny 8321, not Cannapproach. Cannapproach is Michael Nguyen's R&D environment for the CCMS product. None of the writes here go through CCMS today — every recommendation is an AROYA SPA setting.

If CCMS later gets bound to Cannapproach

The CCMS guardrails clamp absolute ranges and rate-of-change (max 2°F/write, max 0.15 kPa VPD/write, max 30 min P0-delay/write, max 12 writes/day per datapoint). They do not catch AROYA-side contradictions like the panic-floor/target-min identity. So fixing the AROYA-side issues above becomes more important once CCMS is in the loop, not less.

Per the project, CCMS reads can come from the controller directly via GET /homes/monitoring/ on its LAN — but the steering loop still writes AROYA target ranges as truth.
