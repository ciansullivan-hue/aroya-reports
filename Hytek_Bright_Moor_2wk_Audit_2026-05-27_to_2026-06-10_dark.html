This is the opening POC read of Hytek: Bright Moor Gardens; there is no prior AROYA audit or commitment history for this site, so this section sets the baseline rather than grading deltas. The deck is already steering coherently at the finish — the ripening pair is a textbook generative-finish cohort and should be treated as the in-house template. The three things the next two weeks should standardize, in priority order:
1 · Normalize the bulking recipe. Daily dryback spans 9.3–17.3 pp and peak VWC 31.3–41.9% across rooms that read as the same phase — pick one P1 onset, shot size and P2 count per phase recipe and program it identically (§7, §9).
2 · Resolve the early-flower split. The three stretch rooms span 18.5 pp of dryback (10.3–28.8 pp) — confirm whether they are genuinely the same phase, and if so converge their programs (§5, §7).
3 · Close two telemetry gaps so the next audit can grade more. (a) No CO₂ sensor is deployed — CO₂ dosing is invisible to AROYA; adding an EE894/CO₂ probe in flower unlocks the climate side. (b) No flow/runoff series exists, so shot sizes and runoff % are derived, not measured — a drain-sensor or a one-week manual runoff log against the 20–30% bulking target would let the next cycle grade leaching directly. (c) Substrate type is not set in the AROYA room config — setting it tightens every phase target band.

  • Shot counts and P1/P2 cadence are DERIVED, not measured — a "shot" is a VWC step-up of ≥0.8 pp between consecutive ~9-min samples on the zone-mean series. No flow-meter or valve-event telemetry was available. Counts are reliable for rhythm/consistency comparison; absolute shot sizes (% of substrate volume) are not graded.

  • Rooms excluded from the deep-dive — Veg 1 (6888) returned no chart data this window (single device, likely offline/unplaced) and Veg 3 (6901) carries climate sensors only (no substrate probes).
