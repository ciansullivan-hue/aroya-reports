Prioritized, plain-language. Owner = facility lead; Grower = day-to-day room operator; Process = AROYA / Addium follow-up. Each ties back to the data and the declared phase target.Priorizadas. Owner = responsable; Grower = operador; Process = AROYA / Addium.

Grower FR 3 (Ripening/Generative 2) — deepen the dry-down toward the ripening band
FR 3 is declared Ripening/Generative 2 but only dries back ~5.1 pp absolute against a 25–35 pp ripening target. Lengthen P3 (push the last shot earlier / cut a late P2 pulse) so the room earns the generative dry-down you set for it.
Ripening is where the deep, controlled dry-down drives metabolite finish and pulls moisture out of the bud. A ripening room running a shallow dryback is steering vegetative against a generative recipe — the clearest intent-vs-data mismatch in the facility, and the highest-quality-leverage one to fix.
Grower FR 6 (Early Flower/Generative) — confirm the deep dryback is intended
FR 6 dries back ~37.5 pp absolute overnight — the deepest in the facility — against a 20–30 pp early-flower band. If that's a deliberate generative push, great; if it's a stretched gap between shots, add a light P1 shot or a P2 pulse so the dry-down is controlled, not a free-fall.
A dryback running above even the generative band on a low-CEC soilless block risks salt build-up and root stress between shots. You declared this room generative, so the depth is on-strategy — but it's at the edge, and confirming intent now keeps you from over-driving into the stretch.
Grower Veg room — pull RH down / lift VPD
The Veg room averages RH ~74.1% (peaks 87.0%) with VPD ~0.89 kPa — humid and soft. Target ~0.8–1.1 kPa VPD / 60–75% RH so the plants transpire and pull a clean veg dryback.
Low VPD flattens transpiration, which is why this room's overnight dryback reads more generative (~16.3 pp) than a veg room usually wants — the plant isn't drinking enough during the day. Firming the climate restores a true veg steering signal and reduces mildew risk on dense veg canopy.
Grower Flower rooms — lock the P1 onset clock
First-shot times drift modestly day-to-day across the flower rooms. Pick one P1 onset (lights-on + a fixed P0 delay) per room and hold it so the day starts the same way every morning.
A consistent P0 delay is the foundation of repeatable steering — if the day starts at a different time, the overnight dryback and EC stack drift with it. Same room, same recipe, same clock is the cheapest consistency win.
Process Nursery & Dry Room — confirm the climate heads stay online
The Nursery head came online 2026-06-08 (28.5% window coverage) and the Dry Room 2026-06-02 (71.4%). Confirm both are now reporting steadily (see Device Health) so the next audit has a full window for these rooms.
These are honest device gaps, not cultivation gaps — but a dry room without continuous RH/temp is a quality blind spot during the most sensitive 10–14 days of the cycle, and the Dry Room HG (6/4 Harvest 5&6) is finishing 06-14.
Process Config + next audit — add a runoff signal, set substrate type
There's no flow/runoff telemetry, so shot sizes and runoff % are derived, not measured. A drain sensor or a one-week manual runoff log (volume in vs out + runoff EC) unlocks leaching analysis. Confirm substrate type is set in each room's AROYA config to tighten the target bands.
Runoff EC is the cleanest read on whether your generative EC stack is being leached or held. With it, the next audit can grade leaching events (like FR 6's heavier-shot days) precisely instead of inferring them from VWC.
