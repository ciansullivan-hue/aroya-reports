Climate ↔ Irrigation InterplayInteracción Clima ↔ Riego

Does the day's VPD profile match the day's shot rhythm and EC trajectory? A higher-VPD lights-on period pulls water out faster (higher ET) and needs P2 maintenance pulses to defend field capacity; a calmer day lets you cut P2 and drive a deeper P3 dry-down. Audit-level limitationsLimitaciones
  • POWR 2 & POWR 3 phases are SIGNAL-INFERRED — both rooms are between cycles with no CURRENT harvest group, so their phase labels (ripening/flush, bulking) are read from steering signatures (EC trajectory, dryback depth in pp, photoperiod), not the AROYA calendar. POWR 1 + POWR 4 are roster-verified (Bulking/Vegetative 2).
  • Shot counts are DERIVED, not measured — a "shot" is a VWC step-up of ≥2 pp between consecutive 1-h bins. No valve-event or flow-meter telemetry exists. Counts are reliable for rhythm/consistency; absolute shot volumes (% of substrate) are not graded.
  • POWR 1's window-mean dryback (~42 pp absolute) is span-inflated — it's taken as daily VWC peak − daily VWC trough across the full day on a two-stack room, which conflates intraday swing with the true overnight peak→pre-first-shot pull. Read it as "deep-swinging", not a literal 42 pp overnight dryback. The other rooms are less affected.
  • Photoperiod inference is coarse — lights-on hours are inferred from hour-of-day CO₂/temp ramp; POWR 1/3/4 read ~12/12, Veg Room ~18/6. Good enough for DIF; not exact.
  • POWR 3 drain EC is the strongest "hot zone" signal — zone 57895 drain EC 11.7→14.0 mS (peak 14.9). Drip EC there is ~2.7 mS, so the gap is genuine in-substrate accumulation, not feed.
  • Overnight dryback (absolute pp) = daily peak VWC − the following pre-irrigation low on the zone-mean series. Per AROYA convention, never expressed relative to field capacity.
