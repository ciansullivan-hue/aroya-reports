The Lead — six years in one read

What the lifetime tells us

Klutch Cannabis · ATCPC Ohio is a mature, disciplined indoor program. The account opened on 2020-06-22 with two rooms (Dry 1 and Flower Room 1) and was built out in three deliberate waves: the original five flower rooms came online in 2020, the mid-build expansion (Flower 6–10 plus Mom 2, Veg 2, Clone 2, Cure 2) landed mid-2021, and the back-half flower rooms (Flower 11–14) came online summer 2022. By the end of 2022 the facility was running at its design ceiling — peak concurrency hit 24 simultaneously active harvest groups on 2022-10-23, and HG cadence has held steady at 60–75 cycles per year ever since. The median flower phase has been exactly 63 days for 286 of 334 lifetime cycles (86%) — that consistency is the strongest signal in the dataset that the controls program works.

The cultivar program is bifurcated: a tight workhorse core of Orange 43 (214 lifetime HGs) and Ice Cream Cake (191 HGs) that has carried the room year after year, plus an aggressive testing pipeline — 137 new cultivars added in 2024–2026, and 20+ of those are already running at scale (≥3 HGs in 2025-2026). The shift in 2024 from a 33-strain library to a 170-strain library is the clearest operational change in the dataset.

What needs the meeting time: (1) the harvestedCount field went to zero after 2023 — close-out workflow either deliberately moved to METRC-only or broke. (2) Weight fields (packagedWeight, frozenWeight, finalPackageWeight) are zero on every HG ever — Klutch records weights in METRC only. (3) 130 of 136 TER12 substrate heads are 3+ years old, with 72 of them 5+ years old; staged trade-in to TER15 / Climate One captures accuracy gains and de-risks battery drift. (4) The two ripening rooms in the most recent audit (Flower 8 and Flower 13) carried EC restacks of 3.0–3.8 mS against a 6.0–8.0 mS ripening band; lifetime cadence suggests this isn't a one-off.

Plant-out tracking — the 2024+ gap

Data quality flag — needs a yes/no from Klutch

Looking at the harvestedCount field across the lifespan: 2020 100%, 2021 92.6%, 2022 93.4%, 2023 88.5%, 2024 0.0%, 2025 0.0%, 2026 0.0%. Plant entry counts (plantCount) are populated normally — operators are still recording how many plants enter each HG. But the harvested-out count went to zero starting with 2024 harvests. Two innocent explanations: (a) the HG close-out workflow moved entirely to METRC and AROYA-side closure was deprioritized, or (b) the close-out step is just being skipped at runs end. packagedWeight, frozenWeight, and finalPackageWeight are zero on every single HG ever — confirming Klutch doesn't record weights into AROYA. Substrate sensor age — the trade-in case

The substrate sensor age distribution is the story. 130 of the 136 active TER12 substrate heads are 3+ years old — 72 of them are 5+ years old (Wave 1 + Wave 2 deployment vintage), 58 are 3–4 years old (Wave 3 vintage). Battery health is fine today (mean 90%), but TER12 is end-of-life on the AROYA roadmap and the longer Klutch waits the more aging batteries plus accuracy drift become the rate-limiter.

Process P1 · Data
Reinstate the harvested-count + final-weight write-back in AROYA, or commit to METRC-only and tell us so we stop trying to grade yield from AROYA

Grower P1 · Steering
Lift the ripening-phase EC restack target — at least Flower 8 and Flower 13 in the current cycle, then verify across the next two rotations
Both ripening rooms in the most recent audit ran pore EC at 3.0–3.8 mS against a 6.0–8.0 mS ripening band. This is a 2–3 pp pull-back in feed EC over the last 14 days of bulking → into ripen, not a wholesale change.

Data quality & methodology

Lifetime-level limitations
  • Account lifespan = earliest HG start date in the dataset (2020-06-22, "H:1_F1_6/22-9/29") through the latest HG start date (2026-06-17). The facility's actual onboarding date with AROYA could pre-date the first recorded HG by days or weeks but is not in this dataset.
  • Yields: packagedWeight, finalPackageWeight, frozenWeight, harvestWasteWeight, wasteWeight all return zero on every single HG. Per-room and per-cultivar yield analysis is not possible from AROYA — weights live in METRC.
  • harvestedCount is populated for 2020–2023 HGs but goes to zero starting with 2024 harvests. Plant-survival rate computed only on years it's populated.
  • Per-HG recipe link is not exposed (the recipe field is a boolean rather than an ID), so we can't graph "which recipe was used for which HG over time".
  • Per-room substrate type is "SOILLESS" for every room in the list endpoint — the specific substrate (1 gal coco vs slab vs Hugo) lives in the recipe library and would need a per-HG recipe-to-room mapping to resolve.
  • Device firmware generation (WP4 vs WP5) is not on the portal_api device response — the prior device-health report (2026-06-12) carries this dimension at a per-device level.
  • HG concurrency = derived from startDate → harvestDate span per HG. Resampled to monthly maximum for chart readability. Daily-granularity peak: 24 on 2022-10-23.
  • Phase-duration histogram sums totalDays across each HG's FLOWER-typed phases. n=334 cycles, range 56–77d, median 63d, modal 63d (286 of 334).
  • Sensor age = today (2026-06-25) − device.createdOn from /portal_api/devices/?facility_id=1503. Only ter12 + ter15 substrate models counted in this view.
  • Source endpoints: harvest groups → /harvests/?facility=1503 paginated; cultivars → /cultivars/?facility=1503; recipes → /recipes/?facility=1503; rooms → /rooms/?facility=1503; devices → /portal_api/devices/?facility_id=1503; org → /portal_api/organizations/1186/; facility → /portal_api/facilities/1503/. All hit with the SPA Bearer token from ~/.aroya/spa_token_app.json on 2026-06-25.
  • Active-HGs definition: currentPhase != null in the /harvests/ payload. 17 HGs match (12 in flower-room phases, 3 in Clone 2, 2 in Veg 2).
  • Cultivar-per-year HG count: a multi-strain HG counts once toward each strain in the block. This is the right normalization for "which strains are running in the facility this year" but inflates per-strain counts vs a single-strain-only metric.
