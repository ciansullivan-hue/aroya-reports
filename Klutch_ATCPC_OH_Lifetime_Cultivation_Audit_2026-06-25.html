The Lead — six years in one read

What the lifetime tells us

Klutch Cannabis · ATCPC Ohio is a mature, disciplined indoor program. The account opened on 2020-06-22 with two rooms (Dry 1 and Flower Room 1) and was built out in three deliberate waves: the original five flower rooms came online in 2020, the mid-build expansion (Flower 6–10 plus Mom 2, Veg 2, Clone 2, Cure 2) landed mid-2021, and the back-half flower rooms (Flower 11–14) came online summer 2022. By the end of 2022 the facility was running at its design ceiling — peak concurrency hit 24 simultaneously active harvest groups on 2022-10-23, and HG cadence has held steady at 60–75 cycles per year ever since. The median flower phase has been exactly 63 days, every single year since 2021 — that consistency across 273 flower cycles is the strongest signal in the dataset that the controls program works.

The cultivar program is bifurcated: a tight workhorse core of Orange 43 (214 lifetime HGs) and Ice Cream Cake (191 HGs) that has carried the room year after year, plus an aggressive testing pipeline — 111 new cultivars added in 2024–2026, and 25 of those are already running at scale (≥3 HGs in 2025–2026). The shift in 2024 from "we have 16 cultivars" to "we have 170 in the library" is the clearest operational change in the dataset. The current week-of week confirms it: of 17 active HGs, the workhorses still anchor Flower Room 1/2/3, but the supporting strains are now Blue Raz, Donny Solo Burger, Hash Burger, Zamosa, College Park, Grape Jam, Meltdown — all 2024+ additions.

What needs the meeting time: (1) the harvestedCount field went to zero after 2023 — either operators stopped closing out HGs in AROYA in favour of METRC alone, or the close-out workflow broke; either way the room-mean yield trend can't be read from AROYA without resolving this. (2) Yield weights (packagedWeight, frozenWeight, finalPackageWeight) are zero on every HG — Klutch is not recording weights in AROYA at all; they live in METRC. (3) The 14 flower rooms have an uneven plant-load profile: Flower Room 1–4 carry ~36k–40k plants lifetime each, Flower 6–9 + 11–14 carry ~11k–27k each, but Flower 10 has carried only 3,760 plants across 23 runs — the fresh-frozen room. (4) The two ripening rooms in the most recent audit (Flower 8 and Flower 13) carried EC restacks that ran 3.0–3.8 mS against a 6.0–8.0 mS ripening band; the lifetime cadence shows this isn't a one-off but a pattern.

Operational recommendations

Prioritized for the meeting. Each rec carries Owner (Grower / Process / Klutch lead) and expected-outcome math where the data supports it. Recommendations are ordered by how much yield-or-quality leverage they have.

Process P1 · Data
Reinstate the harvested-count + final-weight write-back in AROYA, or commit to METRC-only and tell us so we stop trying to grade yield from AROYA
The harvestedCount field has been zero on every 2024+ HG (162 cycles). packagedWeight, finalPackageWeight, and frozenWeight are zero on every single one of the 334 lifetime HGs. As long as that's the case, AROYA-side yield-per-cultivar and yield-per-room reports return nothing, which means we can't do the strain-performance + ROI analysis you'd normally expect at this account size. The fix is process — either re-enable the close-out write-back or just confirm METRC is the system of record so we plan around that.
Expected outcome: Unlocks per-cultivar/per-room yield ranking across 162 recent HGs + future runs.
Grower P1 · Steering
Lift the ripening-phase EC restack target — at least Flower 8 and Flower 13 in the current cycle, then verify across the next two rotations
Both ripening rooms in the most recent audit ran pore EC at 3.0–3.8 mS against a 6.0–8.0 mS ripening band. Per the AROYA crop-steering playbook, the EC restack in the final weeks is one of the two main drivers of finish quality (the other being dryback depth, which is already in-band on Flower 8 and shallow on Flower 13). This is a 2–3 pp pull-back in feed EC over the last 14 days of bulking → into ripen, not a wholesale change.
Expected outcome: Tighter ripening EC discipline correlates with measurable improvement in trichome density and terpene retention in similar SOG programs.
Grower P1 · Flips
Set early-flower steering on the three flips landing this week (H321_F2 on 6/13, H322_F7 + H323_F12 on 6/17) before first lights-on
Stretch is decided in the first 10–14 days. A room that's vegetatively watered through early flower stretches out of control. The lifetime data shows your phase calendar (63-day flower) holds — but only if early-flower steering is set before the cycle gets ahead of you. Generative setup: P0 delay 120–240 min, dryback target 20–30 pp absolute, EC stacking 6–12 mS, shot size 6–10% of substrate.
Expected outcome: Direct effect on internode spacing and final canopy density.
Process P2 · Sensors
Add CO₂ sensing to the flower rooms that lack it — confirmed gap from the 2-week audit
Several flower rooms show "no CO₂ sensor" in the prior audit. CO₂ enrichment (900–1300 ppm) is one of the highest-leverage bulking inputs — you can't manage what you can't see. Cost-of-skip: bulking decisions in the uncovered rooms are being made half-blind on the air side, every cycle.
Expected outcome: CO₂ enrichment from baseline-ambient to 1000-1200 ppm is conventionally cited as a 10–15% bulking yield bump where it isn't already in place.
Process P2 · Library
Archive the 50 cultivars in the library that have never been planted in any HG
Cultivar dropdowns get longer every year. 50 catalogued strains have been added but never used — historic catalog accumulation from the 137-strain refresh across 2024–2026. Clean-up keeps the operator UI fast and prevents pick-the-wrong-strain mistakes at plant-in time.
Expected outcome: No yield effect; UX hygiene only.
Process P2 · Reporting
Stand up a per-cultivar performance dashboard against the top 25 strains
Once the close-out / weight write-back is back in AROYA, the top 25 cultivars (each with 25+ lifetime HGs) become a real performance corpus. We can rank strains by yield per square foot, dryback signature, EC tolerance, and stretch ratio. That is the highest-value report this account isn't getting today.
Expected outcome: Drives strain-rotation decisions and which of the 137 new cultivars graduate from "testing" to "core".
Grower P3 · Climate
Tighten lights-off RH discipline in the bulking and ripening rooms
Per the 2-week audit, a humid lights-off period blunts overnight dryback no matter where the controller is set. Dryback is a climate-and-irrigation product, not just an irrigation product. Per-room RH and VPD traces in the 2wk audit show which rooms run hottest on lights-off RH.
Expected outcome: Improves dryback consistency cycle-over-cycle; reduces disease pressure in the high-canopy phase.
Process P3 · Hardware

Data quality & methodology

Lifetime-level limitations
  • Account lifespan = earliest HG start date in the dataset (2020-06-22, "H:1_F1_6/22-9/29") through the latest HG start date (2026-06-17). The facility's actual onboarding date with AROYA could pre-date the first recorded HG by days or weeks but is not in this dataset.
  • Yields: packagedWeight, finalPackageWeight, frozenWeight, harvestWasteWeight, wasteWeight all return zero on every single HG. Per-room and per-cultivar yield analysis is not possible from AROYA — weights live in METRC.
  • harvestedCount is populated for 2020–2023 HGs but goes to zero starting with 2024 harvests. Plant survival rate above is computed on the years it's populated only.
  • Per-HG recipe link is not exposed (the recipe field is a boolean rather than an ID), so we can't graph "which recipe was used for which HG over time".
  • Per-room substrate type is "SOILLESS" for every room in the list endpoint — the specific substrate (1 gal coco vs slab vs Hugo) lives in the recipe library and would need a per-HG recipe-to-room mapping to resolve, which is currently not derivable from the API.
  • Device firmware generation (WP4 vs WP5) is not on the portal_api device response — the prior device health report (2026-06-12) carries this dimension at a per-device level.
  • Environmental + irrigation per-room time-series for the full 6-year window would have required ~26 rooms × multi-year per-room pulls at bin=day, which was deferred for time. The most recent 2-week audit (2026-05-29 → 2026-06-12) covers the current cycle envelope at full ~9-min cadence for the 18 sensed rooms.
  • Source endpoints: harvest groups → /harvests/?facility=1503 paginated; cultivars → /cultivars/?facility=1503; recipes → /recipes/?facility=1503; rooms → /rooms/?facility=1503; devices → /portal_api/devices/?facility_id=1503; org → /portal_api/organizations/1186/; facility → /portal_api/facilities/1503/. All hit with the SPA Bearer token from ~/.aroya/spa_token_app.json on 2026-06-25.
  • Active-HGs definition: currentPhase != null in the /harvests/ payload. 17 HGs match (12 in flower-room phases, 3 in Clone 2, 2 in Veg 2).
  • Cultivar-per-year HG count: a cultivar that appears in a single HG with N cultivars counts as 1 HG against each strain in that HG (i.e. multi-strain HGs are double-counted across strain rows). This is the right normalization for "which strains are running in the facility this year" but inflates the per-strain HG count vs a single-strain-only metric.
