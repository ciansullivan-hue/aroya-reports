RecommendationsRecomendaciones

Prioritized, plain-language. Owner = you / facility lead; Grower = day-to-day room operator; Process = AROYA / Addium follow-up. Each ties back to the data and the phase target.Priorizadas. Owner = usted; Grower = operador; Process = AROYA / Addium.

Grower Grow Room 1 — confirm the phase & steering intent (highest leverage)
Grow Room 1 holds VWC flat near 55% with only a 4.5 pp overnight dryback, pore EC flat at ~3.3 mS, and ~1 shot/day on a ~14 h photoperiod. Confirm against the harvest-group roster whether this is a veg / early room (in which case the read is roughly on-target, just lean on EC) or a flowering room (in which case it is under-steered).
A 4.5 pp dry-down with no EC stack is a vegetative signal. If these are flowering plants, the room is being pushed vegetatively in a window where dryback and EC stacking should be driving quality and weight — the single biggest steering question at the site. If it is veg, no problem, but pore EC ~3.3 mS is at the low end of the 3–5 mS veg band.
Grower Grow Room 1 — lock the first-shot time
Pick ONE P1 onset time (lights-on + a fixed P0 delay) and program it. Right now the first shot drifts ±3.2 h day to day, and some days show only a single shot.
A consistent P0 delay is the foundation of repeatable steering — if the day starts at a different time each morning, the overnight dryback and the EC trajectory drift with it. Grow Room 2 already does this (±0.5 h); Room 1 should match it.
Grower Grow Room 1 — pull RH down & watch substrate heat
Humidity peaks 78.8% (mean 65.7%) and the substrate runs ~79.5°F (ideal root-zone 69–76°F). Lean on dehumidification / airflow, and check root-zone heat from lights / mats.
High humidity in a closing canopy is the classic bud-rot / powdery-mildew setup and it flattens VPD; warm substrate speeds dryback and stresses roots. Both are the clearest environmental risks in this room’s data.
Grower Grow Room 2 — hold the line, sanity-check CO₂
Keep Grow Room 2 on its current program — 17.3 pp dryback, ~10 shots/day, EC stacking to ~7.1 mS and a tight first-shot clock is exactly what bulking should look like. One thing to verify: CO₂ averages 1439 ppm and peaks 2000 ppm — confirm that’s the intended dosing setpoint (~900–1300) and the sensor is reading true.
This room is the template for the site. The only open question is the high CO₂ ceiling — either the dosing setpoint is aggressive or the EE894 needs a calibration check; both are quick to confirm.
Owner Both rooms — set substrate type & add a runoff signal
Set the substrate type in each room’s AROYA config (tightens every target band). Add a drain/runoff sensor or a one-week manual runoff log (volume in vs out + runoff EC) so leaching can be graded.
Substrate type is unset, so the bands here are generic. There is no flow/runoff series, so shot sizes and runoff % are derived, not measured — a runoff signal would unlock EC-leaching analysis, especially for Grow Room 2’s stack.
Process Follow-up — confirm phases & close the Apr mesh incident
Re-pull the live harvest-group roster once the AROYA app session is back to confirm the phase labels in this report. Confirm the April 27 repeater (H3400003153) RMA is closed and the flower-side mesh is healthy (see companion Device Health report).

Audit-level limitationsLimitaciones
  • Phases are SIGNAL-INFERRED, not roster-verified — the harvest-group roster was unavailable this run (AROYA app / SPA session down), so phase labels are read from steering signatures (photoperiod, dryback depth, pore-EC trajectory, VPD). Treat them as approximate until re-verified against a live roster pull. This is the first cultivation read of this site; the prior touch was the Apr 27 mesh-health incident.
  • Both flower rooms are fully sensed (TEROS substrate + climate + CO₂), so VWC, dryback, pore EC and substrate temp are graded in both Grow Room 1 and Grow Room 2 — there is no climate-only room at this site.
  • Shot counts and P1/P2 cadence are DERIVED, not measured — a "shot" is a VWC step-up of ≥0.8 pp between consecutive ~3-min samples. No flow-meter or valve-event telemetry exists. Counts are reliable for rhythm/consistency; absolute shot sizes (% of substrate volume) aren’t graded.
  • No runoff series exists for this facility — runoff % and runoff EC can’t be graded. A drain sensor or a manual runoff log would unlock leaching analysis.
  • Substrate type is not set in the AROYA room config — target bands are therefore generic. The signal (high daily peak VWC, deep drybacks in Room 2) reads like rockwool or a high-water-holding block, but isn’t confirmed.
  • Both rooms have CO₂ sensing, so CO₂ is graded. Clock times are local Central time (CDT, UTC−5), with lights-on estimated from each room’s diurnal air-temp ramp — Grow Room 1’s lights-on estimate is noisy given its flat, near-veg profile.
  • Overnight dryback (absolute pp) = daily peak VWC − the following pre-irrigation low on the zone-mean series. Per AROYA convention, never expressed relative to field capacity.
