Irrigation Shot-Cycle — P0 / P1 / P2 / P3

The 4-phase day is how the controller is programmed: P0 activation (lights-on → first shot — let the plant wake and start transpiring), P1 ramp-up (small shots back to field capacity + first runoff), P2 maintenance (pulses holding VWC at field capacity), P3 dry-down (last shot → next morning — the main steering lever). Shots/day, peak VWC, the field-capacity floor and overnight dryback (absolute pp) are derived from VWC step-up signatures (≥2 pp jumps in 1-h bins; no valve telemetry).

Recommendations

Prioritized, plain-language. Owner = facility lead; Grower = day-to-day room operator; Process = AROYA / Addium / CCMS follow-up. Each ties to the data, the phase target and the prior commitment it closes.

Grower POWR 3 — finish the flush, then re-stack for ripening
The substrate EC flush is working (-3.92 mS over the window to 4.84 mS) on a deep 35.6 pp dry-down. Hold low-EC feed until drain EC confirms it has followed the substrate down (target <6 mS), then taper feed back up so ripening can re-stack to 6.0–8.0 mS rather than free-falling.
This was the top RED from the long-term review and it is finally moving. The risk now flips from over-salting to over-flushing — a controlled taper protects yield in the rooms that carried the most last cycle.
Grower POWR 1 — break the substrate-temp inversion
Restore between-shot oxygenation / increase root-zone air movement to break the inversion (substrate 74.8°F ≥ air 74.1°F). VPD has recovered to 0.92 kPa, so the climate side is largely handled; the substrate temperature is the remaining lever.
This is the one prior YELLOW that hasn't moved. Substrate warmer than canopy air is an anaerobic-root risk and dampens the overnight dry-down on a two-stack room carrying ~700 plants.
Grower POWR 4 — protect the clean generative bulk
POWR 4 is the model room this window: CO₂ steady ~1587 ppm, VPD recovered to 0.82 kPa, EC stacking +0.53 mS to 5.80 mS. Hold the recipe; nudge VPD the last bit toward 1.3–1.4 kPa to lock the bulk.
All three prior REDs on this room are closed. The job now is to not break what's working — small VPD tightening only.
Process · CCMS × AROYA Wire drain-EC + runoff into the live dashboard
POWR 3 already exposes drain-flow telemetry (drain EC / runoff %); surface it in the live CCMS dashboard so a leach can be graded directly instead of inferred from substrate EC. This is the natural first integration win for the CCMS × AROYA implementation.
Two rooms were signal-inferred this window because no current HG was assigned. Closing the loop on drain telemetry turns POWR 3's leach into a measured, auto-closeable item and demonstrates the CCMS integration value immediately.
Process Re-pull the HG roster once POWR 2/3 restart
POWR 2 and POWR 3 are between cycles, so their phase labels (bulking, ripening/flush) are read from steering signatures this window. Re-pull the harvest-group roster once they restart so the phases become roster-verified at the next audit.
Keeps the audit grading against the AROYA calendar rather than inference, which matters most for the rooms that drive facility yield.

Read it as "deep-swinging", not a literal 42 pp overnight dryback. The other rooms are less affected.
  • Photoperiod inference is coarse — lights-on hours are inferred from the hour-of-day air-temp ramp; POWR 1–4 read ~12/12, Veg Room ~18/6. Good enough for DIF; not exact.
  • Overnight dryback (absolute pp) = daily peak VWC − the following pre-irrigation low on the zone-mean series. Per AROYA convention, never expressed relative to field capacity.
