How the WP5 update works & what to do here

The gateway updates to WP5 and pushes it to every device online and communicating at the scheduled time (go/no-go: online, battery ≥ 30%, signal ≥ 50%). Offline devices simply stay on WP4 — not bricked or harmed; the constraint is WP4↔WP5 compatibility, not damage. Updates are normally run remotely.

  1. This facility is fully offline, so there is nothing to push to today. First decide whether the site is being recommissioned (bring devices back online) or retired (trade the sensors in).
  2. If recommissioning: bring the gateway online first (it carries the push), then work the device list — replace flat node batteries and restore signal so each is online with battery ≥ 30% and signal ≥ 50%. Every device up at push time receives WP5; every device down stays on WP4 (unharmed).
  3. If retiring: mark the 36 TEROS-12 and 5 Atmos-14 sensors for a TEROS One / Climate One trade-in in the planner below rather than reviving years-dormant hardware.
  4. Stragglers recover later by reverting a gateway to WP4, bringing the device online, re-running, then returning the gateway to WP5 — no time limit. Keep the mixed WP4/WP5 window short.
  5. Note: existing WP4 gear keeps working as-is; new AROYA equipment requires WP5, which is the reason to update.
