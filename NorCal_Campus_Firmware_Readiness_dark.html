Headline. Of 1917 devices across the six NorCal sites, 507 are online and would take the WP5 update in their building’s window; 1399 are dark and cannot be flashed until physically recovered or retired. The offline population has grown sharply since planning began: the Feb-2026 campus plan logged 330 offline across the five update buildings and the March fleet export showed 510; the live pull now shows 1399 dark. Critically, 105 devices that were online in March have since gone dark, against only 5 recovered — the fleet is eroding while the cable-based update waits.

The update method — cable-based, one building at a time (Gateway Isolation)

WP4 is a legacy protocol that blocks onboarding of any new AROYA hardware, so the campus must move to WP5. The update is a physical, cable-based procedure run one building at a time: every other building’s gateway is powered down so it can’t catch the update, the target building’s online devices are flashed, the result is verified, then the next building’s gateway is powered on. Only online devices receive WP5 — anything dark stays on WP4 and becomes a long-term incompatibility liability, which is why the offline audit below is the real work of the onsite visit (the flash itself is 15–30 min per building once inventory is clean).

Offline-device decision framework

1 · Retire
Decommissioned area, permanently broken, or not cost-effective to recover (best for Bryant). Remove from AROYA — no cost.
2 · Troubleshoot
Active production area and offline < 12 months. Replace battery / check signal and bring online before the flash.
3 · Trade-in
Needed for production but broken/old or offline > 12 months. Add to trade-in quote ($150 off per device), then retire.

Recommended building sequence

  1. NorCal QPC
  2. NorCal SRBD
  3. NorCal 5A
  4. NorCal 4B
  5. NorCal Bryant
QPC first to test the process (best recovery potential), then SRBD, 5A, 4B (most complex inventory), and Bryant last (retire the whole building). GB2 is a 7-sensor data-only site — fold into whichever visit is nearest.
