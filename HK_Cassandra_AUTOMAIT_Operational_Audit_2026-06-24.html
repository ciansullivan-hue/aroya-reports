Critical interpretation rules applied

Rule 1 — Channeling requires evidence beyond a low VWC trough. A deep trough is consistent with aggressive dryback strategy, small substrate volume, healthy transpiration, or any combination. Calling channeling requires drip-side evidence: visible drip pooling on the substrate surface, drip volume vs drain volume out-of-band for the dryback achieved, EC asymmetry across drip/drain that suggests bypass, or multiple substrate probes in the same zone diverging materially. R5's 22.5-22.8% minimum VWC in this window is described in this report as deep dryback / aggressive trough — not channeling. No drip-side evidence is in hand.
Rule 2 — Drip EC > drain EC is the normal, healthy direction. Feed water carries salts in (drip EC). Plants pull water out under transpiration. Salts stack in the substrate. Drain exits with whatever was not extracted. Drip EC normally exceeds drain EC because plants are aggressively extracting salts & nutrients from the feed before runoff. When drip EC < drain EC, the most likely explanation is that drip and drain probes are physically installed in swapped positions (the report flags this explicitly when seen). The prior 2-week audit's labelling of R2 (drip 5.94 / drain 2.86) and R5 (drip 5.84 / drain 2.10) as "flush-through leaching" is retracted — those drip-over-drain magnitudes are exactly what healthy salt uptake looks like.
